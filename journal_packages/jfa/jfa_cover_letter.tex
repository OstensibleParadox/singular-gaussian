I am writing to submit the manuscript ``Singular Gaussian Conditioning via the Inverse-Free Schur Complement'' as an original research article for JFA.

In finite dimensions, Gaussian conditioning is represented by the Schur form
\[
A - B D^{-1} B^*.
\]
In Hilbert-space Gaussian models with trace-class covariance, this form is not a valid ambient operator formula: the complementary covariance block is compact and has no bounded inverse on the full state space. This paper shows that the correct replacement is the Anderson--Trapp shorted operator, understood as an inverse-free Schur construction on the Cameron--Martin range.

Our results provide:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a rigorous shorting reformulation of the block-triangular OU regularization and the rank-one conditioning setup;
  \item the canonical scaling law $\Sigma_{\lambda,kk}=\varepsilon_0/(2\lambda^2)$ and residual estimate
  \[
  S_{k,\lambda}=\frac{\varepsilon_0}{2\lambda^2}-R_\lambda,\qquad R_\lambda=O(\lambda^{-6}),
  \]
  which separates the singular and finite parts;
  \item a Galerkin consistency theory via nested Rayleigh--Ritz truncations, avoiding direct matrix inversion;
  \item Gaussian finite-part asymptotics for rank-one disintegration,
  \[
  \mathfrak{J}_\lambda(\mu_{\mathrm{obs}};\mu_\lambda)=\frac{b_k^2}{\varepsilon_0}+O(\lambda^{-4}),
  \]
  obtained from disintegration and Fredholm determinant/trace cancellations.
\end{enumerate}

We also include a concrete 1D elliptic Gaussian field example to demonstrate the framework in a standard trace-class/SPDE setting.

The manuscript is closely aligned with JFA’s scope through its focus on positive block operators, shorted operators, Cameron--Martin geometry, trace-class covariance, and Hilbert-space variational methods; the probabilistic application is a major consequence of the operator-theoretic core.
